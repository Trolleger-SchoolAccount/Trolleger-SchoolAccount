Simple Light & Quick Spaghetti Recipe

This recipe is for a simple, light, and quick spaghetti dish you can make in a pinch! It's designed for one medium portion, but you can adjust the quantities as needed. Check out the image at the bottom for a visual reference.

Ingredients

  • 4 ounces (one medium handful) of dry spaghetti
  • 6 cups of water (or more, if desired)
  • A pinch of salt (optional)
  • A tablespoon of oil (optional)
  • 300 milliliters of milk
  • 1-2 ounces of cream cheese, feta cheese, or a mix of similar cheeses
  • Tomato sauce (optional)
  • Salt and pepper (to taste)

Directions

  1. In a pot, bring 6 cups of water to a boil over high heat.
  2. Once boiling, reduce the heat to medium and add the spaghetti, along with the pinch of salt and the tablespoon of oil.
  3. If your spaghetti is too long, don't worry! It will soften in the water. Stir every 2-3 minutes to prevent sticking, ensuring the spaghetti is fully submerged.
  4. Cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the spaghetti is soft.
  5. Strain the spaghetti and set it aside in the strainer or return it to the pot.
  6. Add the milk, cheeses, and any additional seasonings to the pot. Mix until it becomes creamy. You can also return the strained spaghetti to the pot at this point.
  7. Combine the spaghetti with the sauce, ensuring the cheese coats the pasta evenly.
  8. Serve in a bowl or on a plate. Top with parmesan, tomato sauce, fresh tomatoes, or any other desired toppings. Enjoy!
